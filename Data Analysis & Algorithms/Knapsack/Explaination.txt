# üß† Fractional Knapsack Problem ‚Äî Easy Theory

---

## üß© What is the Knapsack problem?

Imagine you are a thief with a **bag (knapsack)** that can carry only a limited weight (say 60 kg).
There are several items available, each with:

* a **value** (profit)
* a **weight**

Your goal:
üëâ **maximize the total value you can carry**,
but you cannot exceed the total weight capacity of the bag.

---

## üéí Two types of Knapsack problems

| Type                    | Can we take fractions of items?          | Method               | Example                           |
| ----------------------- | ---------------------------------------- | -------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| **0/1 Knapsack**        | ‚ùå No (either take full item or leave it) | Dynamic Programming  | Take or skip items                |
| **Fractional Knapsack** | ‚úÖ Yes (can take parts of items)          | **Greedy Algorithm** | You can take half or ¬º of an item |

We are solving the **Fractional Knapsack** version here.

---

## ‚öôÔ∏è What does ‚ÄúGreedy Method‚Äù mean here?

It means:
At each step, **take the item that gives the maximum value per weight unit** (called *value/weight ratio*).
You keep doing this until your bag is full.

So the greedy rule is:

[
\text{Pick items in descending order of } \frac{\text{value}}{\text{weight}}
]

---

## üí° Example (simple to understand)

Suppose we have:

| Item | Value | Weight | Value/Weight |
| ---- | ----- | ------ | ------------ |
| 1    | 60    | 10     | 6            |
| 2    | 100   | 20     | 5            |
| 3    | 120   | 30     | 4            |

Knapsack capacity = 50

### Step 1: Sort by ratio (v/w)

Order becomes:
Item 1 ‚Üí 6, Item 2 ‚Üí 5, Item 3 ‚Üí 4

### Step 2: Take greedily

| Step                | What we take                           | Remaining capacity | Total value               |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------------- | ------------------ | ------------------------- |
| Take item 1 (10 kg) | Full                                   | 40                 | 60                        |
| Take item 2 (20 kg) | Full                                   | 20                 | 160                       |
| Take item 3 (30 kg) | Take 20 kg out of 30 kg (2/3 fraction) | 0                  | 160 + 120 √ó (20/30) = 240 |

‚úÖ **Maximum Value = 240**

---

## üßÆ Algorithm (step-by-step summary)

1. **Input:** List of `n` items, each with `value` and `weight`, and knapsack capacity `W`.
2. **Compute ratio:** For each item, find `ratio = value / weight`.
3. **Sort items** in **descending order** by ratio.
4. **Initialize** `totalValue = 0`.
5. For each item:

   * If item fits completely (`weight ‚â§ remaining capacity`)
     ‚Üí take full item.
   * Else
     ‚Üí take only the fraction that fits (`value √ó (remaining capacity / weight)`).
   * Stop when capacity = 0.
6. **Output totalValue** as the maximum profit.

---

## ‚è±Ô∏è Time & Space Complexity

| Step         | Time                             | Reason                    |
| ------------ | -------------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Sorting      | O(n log n)                       | Sort items by ratio       |
| Taking items | O(n)                             | One pass through items    |
| **Total**    | **O(n log n)**                   | Dominated by sort         |
| **Space**    | **O(1)** (excluding input array) | Only a few variables used |

---

## üß† Why it‚Äôs Greedy & Optimal

* It‚Äôs greedy because at every step we make a **locally best choice** (highest value/weight ratio).
* For this problem, greedy gives a **globally optimal** solution (unlike 0/1 Knapsack where it doesn‚Äôt always work).

‚úÖ **Summary you can write in your journal file**

**Aim:**
To solve the fractional knapsack problem using the greedy method.

**Algorithm:**

1. Compute value/weight ratio for each item.
2. Sort items in decreasing order of ratio.
3. Take items until capacity allows; if remaining capacity < item weight, take fractional part.
4. Calculate total value.

**Output:**
Maximum value that can be obtained.

**Complexity:**
Time = O(n log n), Space = O(1).
